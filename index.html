Internship

My internship was based on predicting characteristic traits, not for individuals but for business entities, an innovative idea that I conceptualised. A business ecosystem is a purposeful business arrangement between two or more entities to create and share collective value for a common set of customers. Present day scenario demands a company to manually check particular requirements that they would like to be fulfilled in order to get into a collaborative ecosystem with other existing companies. Our end goal is to create an automated recommender system which will provide a value addition ecosystem for the companies. We expect the system to segregate the companies into their respective fields and predict the potential companies in its ecosystem.

Each individual company will be left with a list of potential ecosystem partners that they can collaborate with to get their expected value out of the collaboration. In future, the system will be further developed into a platform with the recommendation system to get the 360-degree business value by offering it as a product or service platform.

Research Project

The idea to find systems that can give us an edge in large computations pushed me to work on this topic. Training and inference of deep neural networks are computationally intensive. The computational intensity and memory demands of deep learning increase proportionally with increase in size of datasets and complexity of DNNs. This requires a very efficient implementation to bring the training time down to a reasonable level while preserving the inference accuracy. This report analyses a distributed training of a residual neural network architecture used for image classification. The focus lies on the implementation of such training with parallelism techniques. The architecture is parallelized across the model's input data and the neural network layers, namely Data and Model parallelism. PyTorch and message passing interface protocol are considered for the execution of these methods. The report summarizes the findings of such practices when executed with a training dataset of 50,000 images on a CPU cluster and GPU cluster. Parameters such as training time, training accuracy, and loss are the sole contributors to deriving conclusions.

AIoT Project

The concept proposed dealt with using AI systems in detecting leaks which can be more efficient and less expensive than traditionally available methods. 20-30% of a compressor’s output can be leaked which can account for a significant source of wasted energy in an industrial compressed air system. Our end goal is to built a system that utilizes AI-based sound analysis to detect leakages in pneumatic systems with a goal to evaluate Holistic DevOps and AI/Machine learning model reuse.Currently, 4 test systems are deployed around the globe, including an installation at the Bosch Connectory in Chicago, the Bosch research facility in Shanghai, the TÜV Süd test lab in Singapore, as well as the lab HQ in Heilbronn, Germany.
